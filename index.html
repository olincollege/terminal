terminal

A Terminal-Based Murder Mystery Game

🕯️ How it all began...

With a thump on my doorstep in the middle of the night.

My sister died, twenty years ago. And now, twenty years later, I've just received a package in the mail. It was a ratty old thing, large and clunky, taped together with a peeling, browning adhesive that I was willing to bet was about as old as me. It crumpled at my touch-almost like it had been waiting for me.

Inside was something even stranger: squat and square, with a dim, six-by-eight inch pixelated screen-it was her computer. Her final message. Her last act. You see, she wasn’t just my sister—she was a government cybersecurity specialist for the FBI. And her death? Never quite made sense to me.

This is my investigation.

🎥 Watch Terminal Come to Life

The machine hums. The screen flickers. Somewhere in the code, her voice lingers.

This demo walks through the first steps of the investigation — navigating her encrypted directories, decoding old journal entries, and breaking past the first firewall.

🔍 Unique Features

  • Immersive terminal-based file explorer UI
  • Unlockable file layers via password-cracking
  • Rich, hidden narrative through diary entries, contracts, and lab logs
  • Built-in image viewer for uncovering visual evidence

⚙️ Installation

Ensure you have Python installed. Then run:

git clone git@github.com:olincollege/terminal.git
cd terminal
pip install -r requirements.txt
python main.py

For full details, refer to the README.
